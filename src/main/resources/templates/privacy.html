Privacy notice for Get legal aid data

The Ministry of Justice (MoJ) is committed to the protection and security of your personal information. This privacy notice explains how we collect, use, and store personal data when you use this service. It applies only to this internal service.

MoJ is the data controller for any personal data processed through this service.

The type of personal data we process

We do not directly collect any personal information from our users when accessing this site. The information we do process is collected indirectly through system-level logging:

IP address and device detailsare captured automatically by our ingress logging process. This is part of the infrastructure that manages secure access to the service. Ingress logs record technical details about requests to the system, such as the originating IP address, browser type, and device information.

We use these logs to:

  • maintain the security and integrity of the service
  • detect and prevent unauthorised access or malicious activity
  • monitor system performance and troubleshoot technical issues

This data is not used to identify individuals for any other purpose and is only processed for operational and security reasons.

No special category data (such as health, ethnicity, or criminal records) is collected.

How we get your personal data and why we have it

Most of the personal information we process is provided directly by users when accessing this service. We use this data for analytics and metrics to improve internal services.

Who we share your data with

We do not share your data with other government departments or third parties. No external vendors or cloud hosting providers are involved.

Lawful basis for processing

The lawful basis for processing your data is that it is necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest and for internal operational purposes.

How we store your personal data

The data will be kept for as long as the projects it supports exist and for as long as it remains relevant. This is not currently covered by any existing retention schedule.

Security measures

Access controls are in place to ensure only authorised users can access this service.

International data transfers

There are no international transfers of data.

Cookies and tracking

Get legal aid data uses temporary session cookies to keep your session active while you navigate the site. Details of the cookie policy can be found here.

Your rights

This service does not collect or store personal information such as names, email addresses, or contact details. The only data captured relates to technical access, such as session cookies and ingress logs (IP address and device details), which are used solely for security and operational purposes.

Because this data cannot be linked back to an individual without disproportionate effort, most data subject rights under UK GDPR (such as access, rectification, or portability) are unlikely to apply in practice. However, you still have the right to:

  • be informed – We provide this notice to explain what data is collected and why.
  • raise concerns – To exercise any of your information access rights, that are not a request for a copy of your personal information from the Ministry of Justice, you can contact DataProtection@justice.gov.uk.

Review of this privacy notice

This notice will be reviewed regularly. Next review date to be confirmed.
